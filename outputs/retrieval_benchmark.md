# Retrieval Benchmark — Item2Vec + FAISS

Stage-1 candidate generation: given the current cart, retrieve the top-100 add-on candidates from the catalogue for the ranker to score.

- **Catalogue:** 15,000 items · embedding dim 64 (Item2Vec skip-gram)
- **Item2Vec coverage:** 87.7% of items have a learned embedding (1,850 cold items fall back to cuisine-mean / the content-embedding path)
- **Search threads:** 1 (per-request serving latency)
- **Index build time:** FlatIP 0 ms · IVFFlat 57 ms

## 1. Retrieval latency  (top-100, single query)

Measured over 3,000 real cart prefixes on the production all-data index.

| Index | Cart pooling | p50 (ms) | p95 (ms) | p99 (ms) | mean (ms) | max (ms) |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| FlatIP (exact) | mean | 0.164 | 0.212 | 0.371 | 0.172 | 0.576 |
| FlatIP (exact) | last | 0.155 | 0.210 | 0.338 | 0.164 | 0.955 |
| IVFFlat (nprobe=8) | mean | 0.113 | 0.159 | 0.249 | 0.120 | 0.744 |
| IVFFlat (nprobe=8) | last | 0.102 | 0.131 | 0.185 | 0.106 | 0.527 |

> **Target: top-100 in <20 ms — PASS.** Best: IVFFlat (nprobe=8) (`last` pooling) at p95 **0.131 ms** — ~153× under budget. Exact FlatIP suffices at 15K items; **IVFFlat** (`nprobe=8`) is included to demonstrate the sub-linear path that holds latency flat as the catalogue scales to millions.

## 2. Candidate-generation recall  (leakage-free temporal split)

To avoid train/test leakage, a benchmark Item2Vec is trained on the earliest 80% of sessions and evaluated on the held-out latest 20% — measuring how often the item a user actually added next appears in the retrieved set (n=3,000).

| Recall@20 | Recall@50 | Recall@100 |
|---:|---:|---:|
| 0.953 | 0.954 | 0.954 |

**Recall@100 by amount of cart context** — recall stays high across cart sizes and dips slightly for large carts (5+ items), where the easy complements are already in the cart and the remaining completion is a rarer, harder item:

| Cart size | Recall@100 | n |
|---|---:|---:|
| 1 item | 0.940 | 1,094 |
| 2 items | 0.944 | 940 |
| 3-4 items | 0.982 | 842 |
| 5+ items | 0.976 | 124 |

> Recall@100 = **0.954**: the retriever rarely drops a good add-on before ranking (high recall is the goal of stage 1; the LambdaRank stage supplies precision). `mean` pooling captures whole-cart context; `last` pooling reacts to the most-recently-added item — both are exposed to the ranker.
